Discussion

Deliberative systems

Different functions of participatory spaces in deliberative systems:

Mansbridge et al (2021):

  • Epistemic function
  • Ethical function
  • Democratic function

Ercan et al (2019):

  • Expressive function
  • Reflection function
  • Decision-making function

Defects in the deliberative system

  • Tight-coupling: Nodes are coupled too closely together so they will just echo each other instead of complementing each other.
  • Decoupling: Nodes are not connected at all, so good arguments arising in one part does not reach other parts of the system.
  • Institutional domination: When one part of the system - whether deliberative or not - dominates all the others. Or exert undue influence on the others.
  • Social Domination: When one social interest or social class (such as the most wealthy) dominates or exert undue influence on the rest of the system. 
  • Entrenched partisanship: While it can be useful to discuss matters in partisan parts of the deliberative system, each of these parts need to still be open to listen to the other parts for this to have any deliberative value.
